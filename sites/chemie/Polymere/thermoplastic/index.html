The Physics of Thermoplastics

Thermoplastics consist of long, linear or branched chain molecules. Unlike thermosets, they are not permanently cross-linked. Instead, they are held together by Intermolecular Forces (secondary bonds like Van der Waals forces).

These bonds are temperature-dependent. At low temperatures, the material is glassy and rigid. As heat increases, thermal agitation overcomes these weak bonds, allowing chains to slide past each other. This leads to plastic deformation (viscous flow) where the material can be reshaped. When cooled, the secondary bonds reform, locking the chains in their new configuration—a process that is reversible.
