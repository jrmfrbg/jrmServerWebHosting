The Physics of Elastomers

Elastomers work through Entropic Recovery. Unlike metals, which stretch by pulling atoms apart, elastomers stretch by uncoiling long-chain molecules. In their natural state, thermal energy causes chains to vibrate and tangle into "random coils"—a state of high entropy (disorder).

When you apply stress, you force these chains to align, creating order and lowering entropy. The "snap back" occurs because the molecules naturally want to return to their messy, high-entropy coiled state through thermal agitation. This interconnected matrix is held together by sparse covalent cross-links, which prevent the individual chains from sliding past each other permanently, ensuring the material returns to its original shape.
